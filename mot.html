You know that feeling…

…when you sit down to post and your brain goes completely blank?

The brain fog
You open Canva. You close Canva. You try to write a caption, draft six, and post none. The cursor blinks. You make another cup of tea.
The website that just sits there
You paid for it (or built it yourself one Sunday). It looks fine. It doesn't bring in the level of work you want.
The copycat problem
You see a competitor's post get hundreds of likes and think "I could have written that." You didn't. They did. And you can't quite figure out why they look more established, when you know you're just as good.
The referral rollercoaster
Lovely when they come in. Terrifying when they don't. One channel of enquiries and a lot of crossed fingers.

These are clarity problems. When you don't know how to show what makes you different, your marketing can't do it either. None of this is unfixable. The difference is whether you're shouting about the right things, in a way that actually sounds like you.

The Marketing MOT.

A proper look at what you're putting out there, so you stop guessing and start sounding like yourself.

The big chat.

We sit down (virtually) and I ask the questions you've never had time to reflect on. Most people finish this call and say the same thing: I'd never thought about it like that. That's the point. This is 60 minutes, just on you and your business. It's more like a proper business therapy session, except you come out with a plan.

The expert view.

Your website, your socials, how you come across to someone who doesn't know you yet. I go through everything the way a stranger would. What's working. What's putting the right people off. Why the enquiries you want aren't coming in.

Your written guide.

Everything we find, written down and yours to keep. Four things inside it:
  • What makes you different, in words you can actually use
  • Who your ideal client is, their situation, their worries, why they pick you
  • What I found, what's working, what isn't, and why
  • What to tackle first, in order of what will actually make a difference
Open it before you write anything. You'll know exactly what to say.
