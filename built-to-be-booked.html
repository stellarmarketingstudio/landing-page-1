You know you should be posting more often, so you sit down with the best intentions and open a blank caption box. An hour later, you've either got nothing or one draft you're not even sure about. The website that's a bit... meh.
It looks perfectly fine. You either paid someone to build it or put it together yourself over a long Sunday, and on the surface, there's nothing wrong with it. But it isn't actually bringing you enough work.
Looking like everyone else.
An enquiry comes in, and the person mentions they're also speaking to a couple of others who do what you do. Then it goes quiet. A while later you notice they've booked in with someone else, and you never really find out why. You know you'd have done a brilliant job for them.
The referral rollercoaster.
Referrals are lovely when they come in, and a bit worrying when they slow down occasionally. Right now they're your one real source of enquiries, so a lot of your month comes down to crossing your fingers.
The wrong people get in touch.
The enquiries do come in, but they're often from people who want to haggle on price or aren't ready to commit. They've found you, but they're not the people you actually want to work with, because your marketing hasn't learned to filter for the right people yet.
No time to fix any of it.
You know things need to change, but you're not sure what. And even if you did, there's no real time or headspace to deal with it while you're busy running the actual business. So it stays on the list, and the list never gets any shorter.

None of this is a sign your business isn't good enough. It's what happens when the marketing hasn't caught up with the person behind it yet. That's exactly what Built to Be Booked is for.
